Philosophy

Thoughts on craft, process, and meaning.

I have always been interested in how everything belongs together. When I was young, I noticed patterns in nature, in people, and in my own thoughts — shapes repeating, feelings echoing, stories looping back on themselves. Later I learned words like *fractals* and *entanglement*, but the idea was already there: nothing is truly separate. Even when things look far apart or different, they come from the same beginning. My artwork has always followed this curiosity. I don’t try to copy the world as it looks on the surface. I try to show how it feels underneath — like everything is connected by an invisible order.

For a long time, I thought I was just the character I was playing in life: my name, my job, my habits, my fears. But over time I began to sense something larger and quieter behind that character — a deeper self that was watching, choosing, and learning through experience. This helped me understand that being human doesn’t mean being small or trapped. It means exploring one version of a much bigger story. My art reflects this idea by showing many possibilities at once — different paths, layered spaces, and forms that feel alive rather than fixed.

This philosophy isn’t about escaping the world or pretending everything is perfect. It’s about learning how to belong without getting stuck. Just like a crystal can hold a beautiful pattern without trying to control the light passing through it, a person can live fully without being owned by fear, greed, or old stories. My hope is that when someone looks at my artwork, they feel a little more curious, a little more free, and a little more at home in the mystery of being alive — as if they are part of something vast, meaningful, and still unfolding.
