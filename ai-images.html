AI Images

or

Carving with Computers

I start in Stable Diffusion (SDXL) inside ComfyUI, but I treat it less like “image making” and more like concept carving: the prompt is a design brief, and SDXL is my sketch engine. Once I land on an image with the right flow, depth cues, and surface rhythm, I stay in ComfyUI to translate that picture into a depth map—turning the visual into a topographic instruction set where highs and lows are now meaningful. From there it becomes woodworking again: I prep the relief so it will carve cleanly (controlling contrast, smoothing artifacts, keeping edges intentional), then move into BobCAD/CAM to do the real translation from “terrain” to toolpaths—choosing the cutters, stepovers, feeds/speeds, and pass strategy that preserve delicate contours without turning them into chatter or mush. The CNC cuts the bas-relief into walnut, or my wood of choice and then the human part comes back in: brass brush work to open the grain and reveal micro-ridges, sanding to tame the machining signature while keeping the crispness I want, and finally wipe-on poly to lock the surface, deepen the walnut, and make the relief catch light the way it was always meant to. In the end, it’s words → image → depth → toolpath → wood → finish—a continuous chain where each step is a different language expressing the same idea, and the “AI” part is simply one of the tools I use to get to a physical object with presence.
